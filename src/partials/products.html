The history of the milkshake

1880s-1930s When the term "milkshake" was first used in print in 1885, milkshakes were whiskey-based alcoholic beverages. Milkshake has been described as "a strong, wholesome eggnog-type drink with milk, whiskey, raw eggs, etc., serving as a tonic as well as a treat"[1]. However, 15 years later, by 1900, the term milkshake referred to “healthy drinks made with chocolate, strawberry or vanilla syrup.” Around the same time, ice cream was added to the milkshake.
